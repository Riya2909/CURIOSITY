Menstruation

Menstruation refers to monthly vaginal bleeding in women. It is a normal process which lasts for 3-6 days in most women. It is basically the clearance of inner lining of uterus. Women use pads, tampons or menstrual cups during periods.

Contraception

To prevent transmission of diseases during sex, contraception must be used. Contraception is also used to prevent pregnancy. Some of the contraceptives include condoms, oral contraceptive pills, implants, injectables, patches, vaginal rings, Intra uterine devices, male and female sterilization.

Puberty

Puberty refers to sexual maturity in boys and girls. Puberty is the time when teenagers experience various hormonal and physical changes in their body. These are different in boys and girls. In girls, breasts development, hair growth in pubic area and armpits and menstruation begins. In boys, these include voice deepens, muscle grow, facial hairs , hair growth in pubic area and armpits and growth of testicles and penis.

Reproductive Health

Condition of female and male reproductive systems in all life stages is referred as reproductive. It is the state of social, mental and physical well being. Reproductive system is complex system and it must be protected from infections and injuries
